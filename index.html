Cross-Scale Information Organization: An Empirically Grounded Philosophy of Normative Coordination

Author: Robert Mertzman, A.M. (Philosophy), C. Ph. (History and Philosophy of Science)

Institution: Ethics Institute, Saint Petersburg College

Date: August 9, 2025

Abstract

This paper establishes that normative reasoning in logic and ethics operates through universal information organization principles empirically validated across biological, phenomenological, technological, and entertainment media systems. Recent convergent evidence from psychedelic therapeutic research (Carhart-Harris et al., 2018; Erritzoe et al., 2018), bioelectric signaling studies (Levin, 2021), consciousness neuroscience (Deco et al., 2015; Tagliazucchi et al., 2016), and large-scale entertainment media coordination studies (Tekofsky et al., 2013; Winner & Hetland, 2008) reveals hierarchical organizational patterns that provide empirical foundation for philosophical innovation. We propose three core theoretical advances: (1) capability-based propositions that resolve Frege's dilemma through functional equivalence, demonstrated in cross-cultural gaming coordination, (2) temporal coherence functions that ground normative validity in measurable coherence patterns, validated through entertainment media flow state research, and (3) cross-scale markup organization that unifies logical inference and ethical coordination, evidenced through educational gaming and arts integration studies. These innovations address longstanding problems in philosophy of logic and ethics while offering practical frameworks for human-AI coordination, therapeutic intervention, and institutional design with validated implementation models from entertainment media. The framework demonstrates how objective normative coordination emerges from subjective agent architectures through empirically validated organizational principles operating across biological, phenomenological, technological, and entertainment domains.

Keywords: philosophy of logic, ethics, temporal coherence, capability-based semantics, cross-scale organization, psychedelic research, bioelectric signaling, entertainment media

Section I: Empirical Foundation for Philosophical Innovation

1.1 The Empirical Challenge to Traditional Normative Philosophy

Contemporary philosophy of logic and ethics faces a fundamental empirical validation problem that has persisted since the field's formalization in the late 19th century. Traditional approaches to normative reasoning—from Frege's objective propositions to Kantian categorical imperatives—operate primarily through abstract theoretical analysis disconnected from empirical investigation of how actual agents organize information and coordinate behavior. This disconnection has produced elegant theoretical frameworks that nonetheless struggle to explain or predict real-world normative phenomena.

Recent developments in four converging research domains challenge this traditional separation and offer empirical foundation for philosophical innovation. Psychedelic therapeutic research reveals systematic patterns of belief and value reorganization with measurable effect sizes and lasting behavioral changes. Bioelectric signaling studies demonstrate information organization principles in biological systems that parallel logical and ethical operations. Consciousness neuroscience provides quantitative evidence for hierarchical temporal integration underlying normative judgment and coordination. Entertainment media coordination research provides large-scale, cross-cultural empirical validation of normative coordination principles in action.

The Philosophical Significance: If normative reasoning reflects universal organizational principles rather than abstract logical rules, we should find similar information architecture patterns across biological, phenomenological, technological, and entertainment coordination systems. The convergent empirical evidence suggests exactly this—hierarchical information organization operates consistently across scales of natural, artificial, and entertainment systems, providing the missing empirical foundation for understanding how logic and ethics actually function in embodied agents.

Current Philosophical Alternatives and Their Limitations:

  • Experimental Philosophy (Knobe, Nichols, Machery): While experimental philosophy investigates folk intuitions about philosophical concepts, it primarily documents cultural variation rather than identifying universal organizational principles.
  • Naturalized Epistemology (Quine, Goldman, Kornblith): Naturalized approaches ground epistemology in empirical psychology but typically focus on individual knowledge rather than cross-agent coordination.
  • Embedded/Embodied Cognition (Clark, Varela, Thompson): These approaches emphasize the environmental and bodily foundations of cognition but often remain descriptive rather than providing formal frameworks for normative validity.

Our Philosophical Innovation: We propose that normative reasoning operates through cross-scale information organization principles that can be empirically validated and formally modeled. This approach goes beyond documenting variation or describing embodiment to identify universal organizational patterns that enable coordination across diverse agent architectures.

1.2 Cross-Scale Organizational Evidence and Its Philosophical Implications

Philosophical Significance: If normative reasoning operates through universal information organization principles, systematic investigation of belief and value reorganization should reveal hierarchical patterns that persist across different agent architectures and contexts.

1.2.1 Psychedelic Therapeutic Research: Coordination Despite Radical Belief Reorganization

Robin Carhart-Harris and colleagues' REBUS model provides the strongest empirical evidence for hierarchical information reorganization. Their research demonstrates that confidence in negative self-beliefs decreased significantly (d=0.8) following psilocybin treatment, with neural entropy correlating with therapeutic outcomes (r=0.6). Roland Griffiths' studies show that mystical experiences predicted lasting personality changes (d=0.5-1.0) and objective behavioral improvements as rated by family members (r=0.4-0.7).

Entertainment Media Validation: This is validated by entertainment media research (Tekofsky et al., 2013) on multicultural gaming teams, where coordination effectiveness increases when players focus on shared capabilities rather than shared cultural content. This demonstrates that coordination capabilities can persist, and even improve, when content-based identity becomes less salient.

1.2.2 Biological Information Architecture and the Logic-Biology Parallel

Philosophical Significance: If mind and world share organizational principles, biological systems should demonstrate operations analogous to logical inference.

Michael Levin's research on bioelectric signaling provides compelling evidence. In planarian regeneration, bioelectric patterns determine anatomical polarity, with state changes correlating with morphological outcomes at r=0.92. These voltage gradients function as "prepatterns" or a hierarchical tagging system.

Entertainment Media Parallel: This finds a direct parallel in educational gaming, where Learning Progressions (LPs) create coherent instructional sequences. Winner & Hetland's (2008) meta-analysis of arts education reveals a four-component hierarchy that leads to significant gains in language (0.61 SD), math (0.36 SD), and GPA (0.55 SD), demonstrating that hierarchical information organization enhances coordination capabilities.

1.2.3 Consciousness, Temporal Coherence, and Normative Validity

Philosophical Significance: If normativity requires temporal coherence, consciousness research should reveal universal coherence patterns that ground normative validity.

Network neuroscience provides extensive evidence for hierarchical organization in the brain with 4-7 distinct levels and processing timescales from 10ms to 10 seconds. The "experienced present" shows consistent temporal bounds of 2-3 seconds.

Entertainment Media Confirmation: These temporal coherence patterns are validated by flow state research (Csikszentmihalyi, 1990) across various media, which consistently demonstrates the same temporal integration windows and universal neurological signatures across cultures. Global Workspace Theory, validated across hundreds of experiments, is also supported by gaming research showing that the same neural networks enabling conscious access also enable successful cross-cultural coordination.

1.2.4 Entertainment Media as Large-Scale Validation of Cross-Scale Information Principles

Entertainment media provide unprecedented large-scale empirical validation of the organizational principles from the other domains.

  • Cross-Cultural Gaming Research: Tekofsky et al.'s (2013) analysis of 4,035 League of Legends games demonstrates that cultural diversity improves coordination when a capability-based identity becomes salient.
  • Flow State Research: Demonstrates universal temporal coherence patterns across gaming, VR, sports, and arts.
  • Educational Gaming and Arts: Provide empirical validation of cross-scale markup organization principles.

1.3 Convergent Evidence for Cross-Scale Information Organization

The convergent evidence from all four domains establishes that normative reasoning operates through measurable, universal information organization principles. The evidence reveals three core patterns:

  1. Capability-Based Coordination
  2. Temporal Coherence Functions
  3. Cross-Scale Markup Organization

Section II: Philosophical Framework - The Multi-Scale Constructor Theory of Normativity

2.1 From Empirical Patterns to Philosophical Innovation

The empirical evidence points toward three philosophical innovations:

  1. Capability-Based Propositions: Replace Fregean abstract truth-bearers with minimal ability specifications that enable functional equivalence.
  2. Temporal Coherence Functions: Provide empirically grounded normative validity criteria based on measurable coherence patterns.
  3. Cross-Scale Markup Organization: Unify logical inference and ethical coordination through universal information architecture principles.

2.2 Resolving Classical Problems in Philosophy of Logic: From Frege's Dilemma to Functional Equivalence

Our Philosophical Innovation: Capability-Based Propositions

We propose that propositions are capability specifications—minimal ability profiles agents must possess to generate, maintain, and coordinate content. This resolves Frege's Dilemma through functional equivalence rather than shared content. For example, "2+2=4" and "the successor of the successor of 2 equals 4" have identical truth conditions but different capability requirements.

2.3 Grounding Ethics in Temporal Coherence: From Is-Ought to Organizational Optimization

Our Philosophical Innovation: Temporal Coherence Functions as Normative Foundation

We propose that normative validity depends on temporal coherence optimization—the capacity of organizational frameworks to maintain agent identity while enabling adaptive coordination across contexts and timescales. This bridges the is-ought gap by grounding normativity in empirically measurable organizational success criteria. This includes a capacity for prospective identity constitution, where agents can authentically transform from competitive to cooperative orientations while maintaining identity coherence.

2.4 Cross-Scale Markup Organization: Unifying Logic and Ethics Through Information Architecture

Our Philosophical Innovation: Cross-Scale Markup Organization

We propose that logical inference and ethical coordination operate through shared information architecture principles, which we term "cross-scale markup organization." Just as HTML markup creates hierarchical structures for technology, analogous principles operate in biology (bioelectric signaling), phenomenology (consciousness), and entertainment (game rules), unifying logic and ethics as different aspects of the same organizational principles.

2.5 The Multi-Scale Constructor Framework: Formal Integration

These three innovations integrate into a unified Multi-Scale Constructor Framework. An agent's configuration is formally represented as:

A(t) = {BROA+(t), C(t), TCF(t), XSC(t), FOP(t)}

A normative framework's validity is then a function of its ability to optimize the Adaptive Temporal Coherence Function (ATCF) across relevant agents while supporting their developmental trajectories.

Section III: Integrative Applications and Philosophical Implications

The framework transforms abstract problems into practical tools for critical domains:

3.1 Artificial Intelligence Ethics and Human-AI Coordination

Instead of programming AI with fixed values (value alignment), we specify the minimal capabilities required for ethical coordination. For example, a medical diagnosis AI would be developed with capabilities for generating responses that preserve patient agency and coordinating with human professionals, using models validated from gaming research.

3.2 Institutional Design for Temporal Coherence Optimization

Successful institutions are those that optimize temporal coherence across multiple scales. Educational gaming and arts education research provide validated models for institutional design that achieves coordination across diversity while supporting individual development.

3.3 Therapeutic Applications: Moral Development and Identity Integration

Therapeutic success involves optimizing temporal coherence. This means supporting clients in maintaining their identity while developing enhanced capabilities for coordination, cooperation, and moral reasoning, using protocols validated by flow-state and gaming research.

Section IV: Contributions to Philosophy and Future Directions

The Multi-Scale Constructor Framework represents a fundamental advance by providing the first empirically grounded framework that bridges classical problems in logic and ethics with contemporary challenges.

  • It resolves Frege's Dilemma through functional equivalence.
  • It bridges Hume's is-ought problem through temporal coherence.
  • It uses entertainment media research as a unique and powerful method for large-scale empirical validation of philosophical theories.

This approach establishes new standards for philosophical relevance and rigor, bridging the traditional gap between theoretical analysis and practical application.
